Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 26, 2026

Pudding (the "app") is a solid-foods diary for parents. We built it privacy-first: the app does not collect your data and sends nothing to us or any third party.

What data the app holds

You may enter: your baby's name (optional), date of birth, the chosen feeding methodology, which foods have been introduced, reaction notes, and free-text notes. This data exists only to make the diary work.

Where it is stored

All data is stored locally on your device using the system framework (SwiftData). It is not sent to our servers — the app has no backend and no user account.

We do not collect data. No analytics, no ads, no trackers, no third-party SDKs. We do not track you across apps or websites and do not share data with brokers. In App Store terms, this is "Data Not Collected."

Notifications

The app may send local reminders (e.g. time to repeat an allergen). They are generated and shown on your device; you can grant or deny permission — the app works fully without them.

Child health data

Reaction notes are sensitive. They are also stored only on your device, never transmitted, and never used for ads or analytics. The app does not use HealthKit.

Deleting data

You can delete an entry inside the app at any time. To remove all data, delete the app from your device. Since there are no servers, no "server-side" deletion is needed.

Children

The app is intended for parents, not for children. Data about a child is entered by the parent and never leaves the device.

Website analytics

This website (the landing page) uses privacy-friendly, anonymous visit analytics (Vercel Web Analytics): no cookies, no user identification, and no personal data. This applies to the website only — the app itself still collects no data.

Future changes

If we add iCloud sync (CloudKit), we will update this policy and the App Store privacy disclosure before releasing that feature.
